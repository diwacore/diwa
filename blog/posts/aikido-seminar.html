I attended an Aikido seminar!

Here I am with some of my PE3 Aikido classmates, our Sensei Ian, and Shihan Ito! The seminar was held last May 11-12, 2024 and was led by Makiling Aikido International where they invited Shihan Makoto Ito (6th Dan) from Hombu Dojo, Tokyo Japan.

I attended mostly because I'm currently taking a higher level Aikido class and got a 2.0 on the midterm exam. The exam was so embarrassing, by the way. I was so nervous that I just froze up and forgot EVERYTHING I was supposed to do. Anyways, it really helps to just be able to do the moves and practice them so I blocked out my Saturday and went.

I didn't really learn anything new; mostly just got to spar with higher-ranked people to practice (and polish) what I did know. I sucked it up and onegai'd my way to sparring with a couple of black and brown belts. It was great!

By the way, if you have at least 2 PEs and 2 semesters left, I genuinely recommend taking PE2 AKD and then PE3 AKD (preferably, in the same school year as PE3 is seasonal to the 2nd sem). Sensei Ian is a very nice, passionate, and kind teacher, and his PE classes gave me a lot of stability through the sems I took them in. Also, after paying for the registration fee to the MaAI federation, passing the PE3 final exam also officially makes you a senior white belt!

I'm writing this blog post a week from my final exam, and I've decided to attend the UP Aikido Club's Sunday session to review and hopefully avenge my 2.0 midterm. Wish me luck so that by this day next week, I'll be a senior white belt!
